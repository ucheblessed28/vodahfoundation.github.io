About Us

WHO WE ARE

Vodahfoundation.org is the official website for Help for Victims of Matrimonial Abuse Foundation. We are committed to reducing domestic violence to its barest minimum. The founders have at some point been victims and survivor of Intimate partner violence in the hands of their ex- spouses. All through the period, they sought help for several years and there was absolutely no help and support available. Because there was no help and support available for them and their small children, they had to do everything humanly possible for the safety their small children and they themselves.

HELP FOR VICTIMS OF MATRIMONIAL ABUSE FOUNDATION



Help for Victims of Matrimonial Abuse Foundation (HVMAF) is a registered Non-Governmental Organization registered in countries around the world with its Africa’s headquarters in Nigeria (CAC/IT/NO 97203). The Foundation which started in the UK as Help for Male Victims of Matrimonial Abuse is duly recognized by the Royal Family and have been awarded with the Royal Recognition Award by Prince Andrew, Duke of York.

Our primary objective is to provide victims of Matrimonial Abuse personal support to overcome the psychological, emotional and physical abusive situation that they are facing. Our aim is to relieve the distress and suffering experienced by woman, children and men who have been maltreated by an intimate partner.

We have also created an experiential and grassroots sensitization, enlightenment and educative Mass Media Campaign tagged SCREAM WORLD. This initiative has been designed to make sure we take the fight against DV from just people’s Eyes to their Hearts.

We also provide free holistic one to one counseling using person cantered approach, offer anger management programs for male/female perpetrators. We find a subtle way to making sure they get their lives back.

  • To relieve the distress and suffering experienced by men, women, and children who have suffered matrimonial abuse.
  • To offer support and assistance to men women and children experiencing family violence
  • To educate the public and partner agencies in the cause and effects of intimate families’ violence against children
  • To work together with other non-governmental organization and government agencies to take violence against children
  • To collaborate with other non-governmental organization with similar objectives and with government agencies to STEM the scourge of domestic abuse
  • To act as caregivers to women, men and children
All of our work is based on respect for the inherent worth and dignity of all people. We are committed to the development that meets the needs of the present situation. We believe that our work will be enduring as we genuinely live and breathe our values.

PROVIDING TIMELESS INITIATIVES TO HELP VICTIMS OF Domestic Violence and SCREAM IT OUT FROM OUR PLANET



Our Approach

We offer psychological, emotional and physical support and assistance to both women and children experiencing family domestic violence. We aim to educate the public and partner agencies in the causes and effects of family domestic violence and ending such violence against couples and their young children.

We will also provide free holistic one to one counseling using ‘’person cantered approach, offer anger management programs for male/female perpetrators. As time goes on, we intend to provide emergency shelter and help if any victim is homeless due to domestic abuse. If they must leave their homes because of violence, abuse or threatening behaviour, we intend to offer help to find a safe place to stay.

We also run a monthly support group meeting and intervention for couples seeking to enhance the quality of their relationship, communication, and shared commitment to safer behaviours which includes domestic violence perpetrators programme. It’s a platform woman can meet and share their experiences with others who have gone through similar issues in hope of encouraging each other. Also, to run the ‘’Men2gether’’ monthly support group meeting respectively using the same approach to achieve similar outcome measures.
